Witness-Separability: The Operational Foundation

The key concept is witness-separability. A gate set is witness-separable if every gate has at least one mechanism that violates only that gate — becoming viable only when exactly that gate is relaxed, not when any other gate is relaxed. This is the operational test of genuine independence.

Theorem 1: Factorization is Inevitable

Factorization Inevitability Theorem

If a gate set is witness-separable, then admissibility in the tested domain irreducibly factorizes into independent constraint channels. This factorization has three necessary properties:

  • Irreducibility: No gate is implied by the conjunction of the remaining gates.
  • Channel-specific unlock: Each gate has witnesses whose viability is unlocked by relaxing that channel alone — not by relaxing any other single channel.
  • Orthogonal failure modes: The failure channels are pairwise distinguishable by witnesses. For i ≠ j, witness wᵢ lies in Fᵢ(G) but not Fⱼ(G).

Corollary: A monolithic constraint cannot reproduce this channel structure. Channel information is structurally irreducible.

Theorem 2: The DAG Correspondence

Any genuinely independent new gate discovered in an extended domain must correspond either to refinement of existing DAG-level invariant classes, or to extension of the substrate model itself. In neither case does it falsify the factorization principle. This makes the theory refine-able rather than brittle under discovery.
