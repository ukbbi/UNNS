Core Question

Does explicit irreversibility—mode commitment via collapse operator ω—unlock utility islands in recursive operator grammars?

Empirical Motivation

XLI and XLI-B established that:

  • Admissibility saturates at ~30-35% under all enrichments
  • Projection improves with asymmetry but remains subcritical
  • Utility correlates with selection (r≈0.72) but caps below islands

This rules out: pure selection, kernel length alone, resonance amplification alone.

New Primitive: ω (Collapse Operator)

ω is a grammar operator that: • Evaluates phase-binned mode distribution (K=8 bins over [0,2π)) • Selects exactly one mode according to its rule • Suppresses all other modes irreversibly • Projects state onto selected mode • Continues recursion (suppressed modes cannot re-enter) ωᵣ (Random): Uniform random mode selection ωᵍ (Guided): Argmax amplitude, tie-break on lowest entropy

Hypotheses

H₇ — Irreversibility Hypothesis
Claim: Utility islands require explicit irreversible collapse steps. Reversible grammars remain subcritical.
Prediction: Variants with ω show discontinuous increase in median G∘, potentially exceeding 15%.
Falsifier: No ω-containing variant reaches utility island criteria.
H₈ — Relevance Asymmetry Hypothesis
Claim: Collapse must be relevance-guided to unlock utility. Random irreversibility destabilizes or remains futile.
Prediction: ωᵍ variants outperform ωᵣ in both projection stability and G∘.
Falsifier: ωᵍ shows no systematic advantage over ωᵣ.
