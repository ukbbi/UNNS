Series Synopsis: From Operator Control to Structural Admissibility

This chamber sequence establishes a fundamental transition in UNNS research methodology. Axis II (Chambers XLV–XLVI) systematically tested whether utility emergence could be controlled through operator-level mechanisms: static asymmetry (ε), coherence regulation (κ), generative events (γ), and their temporal alignment. Through pre-registered experiments with decisive falsification criteria, Axis II was conclusively falsified under preregistered tests.

Axis III (Chamber XLVII) represents an explicit break from operator-based approaches. With all operators disabled (ε=0, κ=0, γ=0), it tested whether utility is a structural admissibility property of specific grammar–topology classes across 450 simulations (9 structures × 50 seeds). Results provide decisive confirmation: utility is topology-dominated and operator-independent.

Axis II Result: Operator-level control of utility is falsified under preregistered tests. Utility does not emerge from operator timing or alignment alone. This result strengthens earlier chambers by excluding operator-driven explanations for utility emergence.
Axis III Result: Structural selectivity confirmed. 3 of 9 grammar–topology pairs admit utility (all DAG topology), while 6 are inadmissible (all tree topologies). Utility is a topology-dominated structural property: DAG topology is necessary for admissibility, while grammar variations (Reference, Pruned, Enriched) modulate frequency (10-20%) but not permission. Hypothesis H₃₁ supported: utility exists as an architectural property independent of operators.
Key Finding — Topology Dominance: DAG topology enables utility through convergence/composition pathways unavailable in tree structures. All tested grammars produce utility when paired with DAG (G1×T3: 12%, G2×T3: 20%, G3×T3: 10%) but zero utility with trees (T1, T2). This 100% topology correlation establishes that substrate architecture determines permission, operators only modulate dynamics.

Methodological Principles: Pre-registration & Falsification

All experiments in this series follow strict pre-registration protocols to prevent post-hoc tuning accusations. Each chamber declares hypotheses, acceptance criteria, and falsification conditions before execution. Chamber XLVI (Axis II.3) exemplifies this approach: single control variable (γ_injection_time), fixed scan grid, binary operators, and three decisive falsifiers (F1: generative dominance, F2: coherence irrelevance, F3: no emergence).

Chamber XLVII (Axis III) represents a fundamental break: no operators, no parameters, no timing. Only discrete structural selection. Success is existential (∃ admissible, ∃ inadmissible), not frequentist. There is nothing to "tune"—only whether the substrate architecture permits utility at all.
