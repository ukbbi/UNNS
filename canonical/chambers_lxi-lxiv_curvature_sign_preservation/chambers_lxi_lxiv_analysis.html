§7 — Cross-Chamber Structural Interpretation

Taken together, the LXI–LXIV data constitute a systematic multi-axis elimination argument. Each chamber was carefully designed to test a distinct mechanistic hypothesis for the structural null. All hypotheses have now been eliminated. The remaining explanation is therefore structural — an algebraic property of the recursive dynamics themselves.

What is ruled out: The curvature null is not due to (1) insufficient coupling strength — LXI shows η variation has no effect. It is not due to (2) coordinate variance heterogeneity — LXII shows that even with a flattening transform, no CERT_NEG emerges. It is not due to (3) spectral rigidity — LXIII shows that gap_collapse cells are all CERT_POS. It is not due to (4) σ_F calibration floor inflation — LXIV-A shows that perfect σ_F homogenization unlocks DEG cells but not CERT_NEG. It is not due to (5) wrong geometric placement of the operator family — LXIV-B shows perturbation moves b_min upward. It is not due to (6) variance accumulation in the admissibility chain — LXIV-C shows minimal effect. It is not due to (7) any combination of (4)+(5)+(6) — LXIV-R7 confirms the null persists under full triple intervention.
Conjectured Theorem: Algebraic Invariant Sign Preservation
Let K be any operator in the admissible UNNS recursion family, and let b(K; β, γ) denote the curvature exponent at simplex position (β, γ). Under the 5σ_F nondeg gate and CI99 sign certification protocol, b(K; β, γ) > 0 for all (K, β, γ) in the admissible domain.

Equivalently: admissible recursion preserves positive curvature. The operator β-γ simplex admits no certified negative curvature under the standard measurement protocol, for any admissible operator family, regardless of nonlinear coupling strength, coordinate variance geometry, spectral gap structure, or combination thereof.

Empirical lower bound: b_min ≈ 0.020 (observed at β=0, γ=1 under A+C intervention, LXIV R5).

The proof strategy should exploit the two submultiplicativity constants established in LXII (both fixed across 20 runs, zero variance): the median C₄₂₂ = 1.353 characterises typical depth-scaling behavior, while the q95 bound q95(C₄₂₂) = 2.136 is the proof-relevant worst-case constant. The q95 > 1 result implies σ_F(4) ≤ 2.136 · σ_F(2)² for ≥95% of cells, which may directly constrain the CI99 interval width from above and in turn bound b_mean away from zero. For a theorem-grade statement, the supremum over all 231 cells should be computed and used in place of the q95.

The LXIII decoupling result — that spectral gap collapse and b_mean are statistically independent across the simplex — is a strong additional structural constraint. If the spectral structure and the curvature structure were coupled, we would expect gap_collapse regions to show lower b_mean. They do not. This independence may be the key algebraic fact that makes a sign-preservation theorem provable: the eigenvalue structure of R(K) is irrelevant to the sign of b(K).

§8 — Secondary Structural Findings

1. Module A reveals calibration artifact in DEG cells. Baseline has 16 DEG cells (cells where L2 ≤ 5σ_F). Module A (variance synchronization) reduces this to 3. This means 13 cells that appeared degenerate under independent calibration seeding are genuinely non-degenerate — they were classified DEG solely because cross-cell σ_F heterogeneity inflated their individual σ_F estimates. All 13 become CERT_POS under A=ON. This is a real calibration finding: σ_F estimation geometry matters and independent seeds overestimate variance for boundary cells.

2. The A+B cross-interaction is inflationary. Module A alone gives σ_F = 0.0666 (lower than baseline 0.0680). Module B alone gives σ_F = 0.0678 (near baseline). But A+B together gives σ_F = 0.0813, substantially above both. The two interventions interact non-additively on the calibration floor. This suggests that the operator perturbation Δ_struct (Module B) introduces variance that interacts with the synchronized seed pooling (Module A) to amplify calibration noise rather than suppress it. This is itself a structural diagnostic finding about the architecture of the intervention modules.

3. The σ_F depth-scaling profile is a structural constant. LXII shows median(C₄₂₂) = 1.353 and q95(C₄₂₂) = 2.136, both with exactly zero variance across 20 independent runs. The q90/q10 ratio = 2.861 is also fixed. These constants are intrinsic properties of the simplex geometry under the UNNS operator — not statistical estimates. Earlier reports mislabeled 1.353 as the q95 due to a decision-object assignment bug (medC422 stored in place of C_unif); this is corrected in the v1.0.0 JSON exports and the updated LXII source. The median is the natural target for analytical derivation from the recursion kernel; the q95 is the proof-relevant worst-case bound.

4. Spectral geometry has a deterministic footprint. LXIII shows exactly 23 GAP_COLLAPSE cells (12 interior) in both independent runs. The spectral collapse geometry is fully determined by the fixed base pool (SPECTRAL_SEED = 888881). This 23-cell footprint is a geometric feature of the simplex — the set of locations where the gate-covariance matrix R(K) has near-degenerate spectrum. Its independence from curvature sign is the key decoupleing result.
